Electrical Components


Pressure-Detecting Doormat

When stepped on, the doormat sends a signal through the Raspberry Pi to trigger the rolling fabric blinds of the key rack. The doormat was built according to the “button” method, which is used to detect pressure. It is composed of two sheets of aluminum foil tightly attached to two firm pieces of board with a 1-inch spacer in between. The doormat operates like a switch circuit: when the sheets of aluminum foil make contact due to pressure, the switch closes, thus closing the circuit. The current is then able to run through the system to the Raspberry Pi, and a step on the doormat is detected.

Trigger weight: >5 lbs


Materials:
  • Cardboard
  • Vinyl
  • Aluminum foil
  • 22-Gauge Wire
  • Felt cover



    • Key Dishes

    The main component of the key dish is the force sensitive resistors. These require pressure to be applied in order to lower the resistance. The circuit is in effect a voltage divider, so by lowering the resistance of the FSR and reaching the threshold voltage of 1.3V makes the pin detect the presence of a key. The 3D-printed frustum shaped key dish and square trays act to contain the keys, so keys stay in the designated areas and on the force sensitive resistors.

    Trigger weight: >30g


    Materials:
  • 3D-printed frustum key dishes
  • 3D-printed square trays
  • Square force-sensitive resistors
  • 20K Ω Resistors
  • 22-gauge wire



    • Raspberry Pi

    The Raspberry Pi functions to receive inputs from the pressure-detecting doormat and key dishes. It communicates with the web server to update who is home when the key dishes contain keys. Additionally, it connects with the motor controller to power the motors with larger current and higher voltage than the standard value of the 3.3V supplied by the Raspberry Pi.
