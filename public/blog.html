Project Update (2019 March 12 - 2019 March 19)

Weekly Objectives

  • Build force-sensitive floor mat out of vinyl, copper tape, and velostat
  • Implement wiring for sensors on breadboard
  • Create frame of key rack

Electrical Component Progress

  • Configured the Raspberry Pi to function with an SD card with the NOOBS operating system
  • Implemented electrical circuit for one key dish pressure pad
  • Raspberry Pi programming:
    • Attempted to program the Raspberry Pi to output 3V3 rail
    • Wrote code to generate infinite loop to test for voltage differences every 10ms

Despite performing testing, the Chi Pledge Class was unable to generate the appropriate response for applying pressure to the pressure pad. The error perhaps stems from the way voltage is outputted, or from the circuit itself. The Chi Pledge Class plans to use the Jacobs Makerspace and Cory facilities to conduct more thorough testing using voltage probes.

In addition, while the velostat was projected to arrive, it was lost in transit during delivery. However, it is projected to arrive within the next few days. From there, the Chi Pledge Class intends to construct the floor mat and does not anticipate any difficulties.

Project Update (2019 March 24 - 2019 March 30)

The Chi Pledge Class members of the Bay Area met in San Jose during spring break to finalize the cuts on the wooden boards and assemble the key rack together and prepare it for gluing.

Physical Component Progress

  • Monday (March 25): Made measurements for the wood boards and dowels and trimmed it to precision
  • Tuesday (March 26): Drilled holes for the pegs, cut dowels, put all the pieces together to make note of changes we need to make
  • Thursday (March 28): Glued together the side panels. bottom, and middle divide.

Challenges

  • Weather: the rainy weather brought about some limitations to us as to where we could work on the project
  • Many of the simple tasks took a lot longer than expected, like taking precise measurements and cutting/sanding dowels. Sanding in particular took a fairly long time in the process, and for the future we could invest in buying our own dowels and accommodating our drills to store-bought ones.
  • Our current process was to make all measurements first, then drill holes and cut the pieces, and lastly assembling all the pieces. However, many of the pieces weren't exactly aligned after we drilling all of them, and this issue could have been prevented if we checked the dimensions/tried assembling the pieces before we drilled each piece. We will end up not using all of the dowels/holes that we drilled.
  • The dowels and wood glue were not strong enough to keep the bottom panels in place, as some of the holes were not aligned properly on the bottom. To make up for this wood screws were used, but one was slightly off and pierced through the side of the key rack. This can be covered up by a hood for the cord connecting to the pressure mat.
