Project Update (March 12, 2019 - March 19, 2019)


Weekly Objectives


  • Build force-sensitive floor mat out of vinyl, copper tape, and velostat
  • Implement wiring for sensors on breadboard
  • Create frame of key rack

Electrical Component Progress

  • Configured the Raspberry Pi to function with an SD card with the NOOBS operating system
  • Attempted to program the Raspberry Pi to output 3V3 rail
  • Wrote code to generate infinite loop to test for voltage differences every 10ms

Despite performing testing, the Chi Pledge Class was unable to generate the appropriate response for applying pressure to the pressure pad. The error perhaps stems from the way voltage is outputted, or from the circuit itself. The Chi Pledge Class plans to use the Jacobs Makerspace and Cory facilities to conduct more thorough testing using voltage probes. In addition, while the velostat was projected to arrive, it was lost in transit during delivery. However, it is projected to arrive within the next few days. From there, the Chi Pledge Class intends to construct the floor mat and does not anticipate any difficulties.



Project Update (March 24, 2019 - March 30, 2019))


Some of the Chi Pledge Class members of the Bay Area met in San Jose during spring break to finalize the cuts on the wooden boards and assemble the key rack together to prepare it for gluing.

Weekly Objectives

  • Mark measurements on all wooden parts
  • Cut all components, drill holes, and hand cut dowels
  • Begin construction of the first iteration of the physical key rack

Physical Component Progress

  • Monday (March 25): Made measurements for the wood boards and dowels and trimmed it to precision
  • Tuesday (March 26): Drilled holes for the pegs, cut dowels, put all the pieces together to make note of adjustments we need to make
  • Thursday (March 28): Glued together the side panels. bottom, and middle divide.

Challenges

  • Rainy weather brought about some limitations to us as to where we could work on the project
  • Many of the simple tasks took a lot longer than expected, like taking precise measurements and cutting/sanding dowels. Sanding in particular took a fairly long time in the process, and for the future we could invest in buying our own dowels and accommodating our drills to store-bought ones.
  • Our current process was to make all measurements first, then drill holes and cut the pieces, and lastly assembling all the pieces. However, many of the pieces weren't exactly aligned after we drilled all of them, and this issue could have been prevented if we checked the dimensions/tried assembling the pieces before we drilled each piece. We will end up not using all of the dowels/holes that we drilled.
  • The dowels and wood glue were not strong enough to keep the bottom panels in place, as some of the holes were not aligned properly on the bottom. To make up for this wood screws were used, but one was slightly off and pierced through the side of the key rack. This can be covered up by a hood for the cord connecting to the pressure mat.

Electronics Project Update (April 1, 2019 - April 2, 2019)


Component Progress

Created working floor mat out of velostat, copper tape, cardboard, and vinyl; successfully implemented electrical circuits for five pressure sensing key dishes; and programmed the Raspberry Pi to handle electrical input and output state changes

Next Steps

  • Implement and wire motors.
  • Solder wires to pressure sensors to match physical component dimensions.
  • Purchase carpet to hide electronic components of floor mat, improving aesthetic appeal.
  • Begin building the second model of the physical key rack after understanding the issues encountered during construction of the first model.

Challenges

  • The previous Raspberry Pi was defective, so the Chi Pledge Class purchased a brand new Raspberry Pi 3 Model B.
  • The Chi Pledge Class performed calculations to find appropriate resistors to allow consistent responses upon applying pressure.
  • The floor mat was registering touch inaccurately and was frequently sensing pressure when none was being applied. Ultimately, the Chi Pledge Class decided to purchase a pressure sensing floor mat as a backup while also attempting to build a second floor mat with a different design.

Project Update (April 3, 2019 - April 9, 2019)


Component Progress

The key dishes and gears have been 3D printed and are ready to be sanded and cleaned. The prints turned out fine, but because of the print resolution of the printer (0.6mm nozzle), there is a lot of refining to do.

Motors

The motors are set up and rotating. They will be attached to the key rack and pull on belts to roll up the key rack veil. There were issues with the gears as the belt continued to slip off of them so new gears with phalanges on the sides to help keep the belt are being 3D printed. In addition, new key racks were designed with higher walls as keys continued to slip off the dishes during testing.

Floor Mat

The floor mat is up and running. The mat consists mostly of reinforced cardboard, with sheets of aluminum foil attached to the cardboard that allows current to flow through when the two sheets of cardboard are pressed together. Reinforcement with gorilla tape ensures that the cardboard doesn't deform after repeatedly being stepped on. Even after thorough user testing, very few deformities were apparent. A fleece cover was sewn to encase the mat to provide more comfort for the user. After we successfully integrated the floor mat sensor to our user interface, we moved on to connect the motors to interact with the Raspberry Pi. The purpose of these motors is that it would power the mechanism that opens the veil and reveal the key dishes behind. All of this would be triggered by the pressure sensor in the floor mat.

Website

The website is finally up and running! What the website does is to display the statuses of residents that live in the dwelling. How this works is through the triggering the sensors of the resident's prospective key dishes, and through the Raspberry Pi, the website will be updated on whether each resident is in the dwelling or not. Meanwhile, updating upon keychain presence in headless mode is being incorporated and improved upon.

Integration

Integration between the physical key rack, door mat, key dishes, moving parts, electrical engineering components, and code is almost complete. Upon pressure on the door mat, the motors will run, causing the curtain to lift up to reveal the four key dishes. Dropping and removing a key/keychain into a key dish will update the respective user's home status on the website. 10 seconds after stepping, the curtain will close. The roof and back of the key rack still need to be attached.
